RESEARCH
Dissertation — Paragons of Political Leadership
How should we evaluate political leadership? What measures bear consequence for assessing
political leadership in practice? My dissertation argues that principled reflection on the
normative character of political leadership is best when the subject is clearly defined. After
providing such a definition, I propose an evaluative framework that harnesses this clarity to
distill the major focal points for assessing political leadership. This framework presents
“paragons,” or ideal types, as a powerful heuristic for approaching the often inscrutable
nuances of political leadership. I outline four paragons of political leadership drawn from the
work of prominent thinkers—Aristotle, Cicero, Machiavelli, and Thomas More. Each
paragon demonstrates the value of the aforementioned framework while highlighting
important principles for evaluating political leadership today.
